Cheesecake

Indulge in the velvety delight of classic cheesecake, a symphony of creamy cream cheese filling atop a buttery graham cracker crust. Each bite harmonizes crunchy sweetness with smooth, rich decadence, making it a timeless dessert for any occasion. Whether enjoyed plain or adorned with fruity accents, this cheesecake is a true embodiment of homemade comfort and culinary elegance, perfect for satisfying sweet cravings and creating lasting memories.

Ingredients

Ingredients for the crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups (150g) graham cracker crumbs (or cookie crumbs like digestive biscuits)
  • 1/4 cup (50g) granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup (113g) unsalted butter, melted

Ingredients for the filling:

  • 4 packages (8 ounces each) cream cheese, softened
  • 1 1/4 cups (250g) granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) sour cream
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C). Grease a 9-inch (23cm) springform pan with butter or non-stick cooking spray.
  2. In a medium bowl, mix together the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter until well combined and the mixture resembles wet sand.
  3. Press the crumb mixture firmly into the bottom of the prepared springform pan, using the back of a spoon or your fingertips to create an even layer. Bake the crust in the preheated oven for 10 minutes, then remove and set aside to cool.
  4. In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened cream cheese and sugar together using an electric mixer until smooth and creamy, scraping down the sides of the bowl as needed.
  5. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Add the vanilla extract and mix until incorporated.
  6. In a separate bowl, whisk together the sour cream, heavy cream, and flour until smooth. Gradually add this mixture to the cream cheese mixture, beating on low speed until combined and smooth.
  7. Pour the cheesecake filling over the cooled crust in the springform pan. Smooth the top with a spatula to create an even layer.
  8. Place the springform pan on a baking sheet to catch any potential leaks during baking. Bake the cheesecake in the preheated oven for 1 hour to 1 hour 10 minutes, or until the edges are set and the center is slightly wobbly.
  9. Turn off the oven and leave the cheesecake inside with the oven door slightly ajar for about 1 hour to cool gradually. This helps prevent cracking.
  10. Remove the cheesecake from the oven and let it cool completely at room temperature. Then, cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or preferably overnight to chill and set completely.
  11. When ready to serve, carefully run a knife around the edges of the pan to loosen the cheesecake, then release the springform sides. Slice the cheesecake into wedges and serve chilled.

Optional: You can top the cheesecake with fruit compote, fresh berries, whipped cream, or chocolate ganache before serving for added flavor and decoration.
